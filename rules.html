Rules
  • Players must CHECK-IN, by signing the roster, for each and EVERY GAME.
  • Teams will consist of no more than 15 players plus one goalie.
  • Players will be assigned to a team; the teams will be as evenly matched as possible. League officials have the right to move players to 'even out' the teams until game five, after game four game rosters will be final barring extenuating circumstances.
  • SUBSTITUTIONS will be allowed under the following conditions: 1-the substitute and the player being substituted are in the same skill group (see below), 2-there are 13 or less skaters, and 3-the substitute is a member of the league. If any of the above conditions are not followed the team with the illegal substitute will forfeit, 1-0. Jon Evans and/or Chris Sciulli will determine skill level.
  • Once uniforms are distributed all players on a team must wear their team's jersey color with a number.
  • A three-minute warm-up period will precede each game. Periods will be 15 minutes, stop time. There will be no overtime period. Games MUST START ON TIME!!!
  • In the unlikely event a ten-goal lead occurs the remainder of the game will be played with a running clock.
  • No checking, full equipment required, must sign waiver/scoresheet each game.
  • Two points will be awarded for each win and one point will be awarded for each tie.
  • At season's end there will be a three-game (per team) play-off as follows:

Total points (two for win, one for tie) will determine the place of the teams for regular season play; ties will be broken by:

  1. Head to head competition.
  2. Total wins.
  3. Division record.
  4. Point differential (total points for– total points against). For league standings, no more than a ten-goal lead will be considered. For example: team 3 plays 4, team 3 wins by a score of 16-4, only 14 ‘points for’ will be credited to team 3.
  5. Coin Toss.

USA Hockey rules will be used with the following additions:

FIGHTING: Fighting will NOT be tolerated. Any fighting where the referee(s) decide a two-minute minor penalty is appropriate an automatic ten-minute Misconduct with a game ejection will be assessed. Any fighting where the referee(s) decide a five minute major is appropriate an automatic Game Misconduct penalty will be assessed, including suspension from the next game. Christine Sciulli, Jon Evans and any referee on the ice at the time of the incident will be final Judges on all Game Misconduct Penalties. Other Game Misconduct or Match penalties are subject to ejection from the league such as: fighting, kicking, spearing, intent to injure, abuse of officials, etc.

Any player who earns two or more Game Misconduct penalties will be ejected from the league, forfeiting all monies.

Any player who makes a nuisance or exhibits unsportsmanlike conduct as reported by a referee, after warning by Jon Evans or Christine Sciulli, may be ejected from the league, forfeiting all monies.

There are no refunds after the first game.
